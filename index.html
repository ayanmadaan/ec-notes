Analysis of Water

  • BOD
    • Used to measure organic quality of water.
    • Measures amount of org carbon that bacteria can oxidize.
    • BOD value is expressed in mg of O2 consumed /L sample during 5days of incubation at 20deg celsius.
    • Indicates amount of dissolved O2 needed by aerobic organisms to breakdown organic material.
    • Values
      • Pristine: < 1mg/L
      • Polluted (moderate): 2-8 mg/L
      • Untreated Sewage: 600mg/L in Europe and 200mg/L in US

  • TOC
    • First chem analysis on oil exploration
    • Amount of carbon based in organic cpd or cleanliness of pharma manu eqp.
    • Measures: total carbon + inorganic carbon (dissolved CO2 and carbonic acids)
    • Total Carbon - Inorganic Carbon = TOC
  • COD
    • Total measurement of chemicals in water that can be oxidized.

COD preferred over BOD cause you get results quickly.

Methods for water softening

  • Removal of Ca2+/Mg2+
  • Sequestration of these ions
    • Bind them to a molecule that prevents them from interfering with a soap.
  • Removal
    • Ion exchange
    • Precipitation method
  • These two ions exist as non-volatile salts, but distillation is expensive at large scale.

Methods:

  • Ion exchange Resin devices
    • polymers containing anions to which Na+ fn group is bonded.
    • Contains Na+ that readily replaces Ca2+/Mg2+.
    • the resin capacity is eventually exhausted
      • contains only Ca2+/Mg2+ for cation exchange
      • SO4(2-) for anionic exchange
      • Cationic resin : conc HCl
      • Anionic resin: soln of NaOH/KOH.
  • Lime Softening (Clark's Method)
    • Add Ca(OH)2 (lime)which softens water (also removes variety of microrg)
    • Ca(OH)2 + CO2 → CaCO3
    • During Lime softening there is substantial reduction in TDS.
    • No significant change in ion exchange.
  • Membrane Softening (Nano filtration or Reverse Osmosis)
    • Remove Ca+/Mg+ as well as other dissolved compounds through SPM.
    • Produces large vol of waste (20% of input steam)
    • Uses substantially fewer and very little solid waste.
